Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 200g pancetta, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook spaghetti in boiling salted water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water and drain the rest.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together eggs and Parmesan cheese.
  3. In a pan, cook pancetta and garlic until crispy. Add cooked spaghetti and toss to combine.
  4. Remove from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture. Add reserved pasta water as needed to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Season with black pepper and serve immediately.

Chocolate Lava Cake

Ingredients:

  • 200g dark chocolate
  • 200g unsalted butter
  • 200g sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 100g all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Grease and flour ramekins.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together in a double boiler.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk sugar and eggs until light and fluffy.
  4. Combine the chocolate mixture with the egg mixture, then fold in the flour.
  5. Pour the batter into the ramekins and bake for 10-12 minutes until the edges are set but the center is still soft.
  6. Serve warm with ice cream or whipped cream.

Kerala Sadya

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup sambar
  • 1 cup avial (mixed vegetables in coconut gravy)
  • 1 cup thoran (stir-fried vegetables)
  • 1 cup olan (ash gourd and beans in coconut milk)
  • 1 cup payasam (sweet dessert)
  • 2 bananas
  • 4 papadums

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice and keep it warm.
  2. Prepare sambar, avial, thoran, olan, and payasam.
  3. Arrange the dishes on a banana leaf in the traditional order.
  4. Serve with bananas and papadums on the side.

Kerala Parotta

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups maida (all-purpose flour)
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Knead maida, egg, oil, salt, and water into a soft dough. Rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into thin layers.
  3. Apply oil, fold, and roll again to create layers.
  4. Cook on a hot griddle with oil until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with curry or chicken roast.
